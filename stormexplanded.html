How to Stay Safe Before, During & After Storms

Knowing what to do can save your life and the lives of others.


Before a Storm (Expanded)

  • Secure outdoor items like trash cans, chairs, and toys.
  • Charge your phone and backup batteries.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with water, food, flashlight, and first aid.
  • Know your shelter location or evacuation route.
  • Bring pets indoors and make a plan for them.
  • Check weather alerts from trusted sources.
  • Fill your gas tank in case of evacuation.
  • Store important documents in waterproof bags.

During a Storm (Expanded)

  • Stay indoors and away from windows.
  • Use flashlights instead of candles.
  • Listen to weather alerts on your phone, radio, or TV.
  • Do not drive through floodwater — “Turn Around, Don’t Drown.”
  • If told to evacuate, leave immediately.
  • Stay in an interior room during tornadoes.
  • Move to higher ground during floods.

After a Storm (Expanded)

  • Avoid floodwater; it may contain chemicals or debris.
  • Stay away from downed power lines.
  • Check on neighbors, especially older adults or disabled people.
  • Throw away spoiled food.
  • Wear gloves and boots when cleaning up debris.
  • Take photos of damage for insurance.
  • Watch out for mold growth in homes.
  • Do not use wet electrical appliances.
